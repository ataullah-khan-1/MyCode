Both my colleagues have explained the technical side really well. I just want to talk about something more straightforward — the real world reality of how we actually work day to day.
Let me just talk about one simple thing — time. GitHub's own research showed that developers finish tasks 55% faster when they use AI tools. That is real. That is significant. In our day to day work that means we can deliver more in the same amount of time. But here is the funny part — if every single time AI generates some code we stop and put it through a long manual review process, we just lost all that time we saved. Think about it. What was the point of using AI in the first place then.
And it is not just about speed. When a developer writes code and then writes tests for that same code, they are only thinking about what they already thought of when writing it. They are not going to suddenly discover something they missed. That is just human nature. But AI tools can generate test cases for all kinds of situations — empty values, null inputs, boundary conditions — things that honestly most of us would not think about in the middle of a busy day. So actually AI is not reducing quality, it is improving it. That is the opposite of what the AGAINST side is saying.
Now if you look at what is already happening in the industry, over 90% of Fortune 100 companies are already using GitHub Copilot today. These are not small teams experimenting. These are large companies with strict security requirements, compliance audits, and serious quality standards. They have looked at exactly this question and decided that yes, with the right automated checks in place, AI generated code is absolutely acceptable. If it works for them at that scale and with those standards, I think we can trust it too.
So for me this debate is actually quite simple. The industry has already made its decision. AI generated code with proper automated validation is acceptable. The only question left for us is whether we move forward with it or keep adding manual steps that slow everything down without actually making things meaningfully safer
